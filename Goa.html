Goa

Once the pride of Portugal's eastern empire, this lush green State with its wide beaches still remains a little bit of "Portuguese Colonial" in many ways. Here Roman Catholicism still remains orthodox, good food and good wine still grace the humblest of tables, life is still full of song and dance and merriment to the lilting beat of guitar and drum.

Highlights of The State...

Bom Jesus Basilica

The Bom Jesus Basilica, perhaps Images's most famous church and among the most revered by Christians worldwide, is partially in ruins but still a model of simplicity and elegance, and a fine example of Jesuit architecture.

Arambol Beach

One of the most laid-back beaches; with plenty of food options, it is perfect for a lazy day. The beach has both a long sandy stretch with soft white sands. To the north of the beach lie a few shacks and rocky outcroppings.

Arvalem Caves and Waterfall

The cave complex is divided into compartments and the 'linga' is enshrined in it which is held in great reverence by devotees. The Shivalinga in the second cave is engraved with an inscription written in Sanskrit and Brahmi characters of the 7th century.

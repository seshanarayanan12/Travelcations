Kerala

Kerala, also referred as “Gods own country” is a tropical paradise of waving palms and wide sandy beaches, this thin strip of coastal territory slopes down from the mountain ghats in a cascade of lush green vegetation. Plantations and forests are everywhere. And canals and backwaters, where ancient craft still leisurely ply the waters, bisect the land throughout the State.

Highlights of The State...

Allepey

Dubbed the ‘Venice of the East’, Alleppey has many backwater channels where you can rent houseboats. Some places worth seeing include Sreekrishna temple, Karumadikkuttan and Champakulam.

Munnar

Known for its tea plantations, Munnar is one of the most visited hill stations in Kerala. Some of the tourist attractions in this paradise include Chithirapuram, Devikulam and Eravikulam national park.

Kumarakom

With alluring flora and fauna as well as numerous lakes, Kumarakom is one of the most serene places in Kerala. Relish a psychedelic sunset on a montage of clouds, or the flying flurry of evening birds.
